# Frequently asked questions

## Is SnapLink free?

Yes — the free tier covers 100 screenshots per month forever. Pro is $5/month
for unlimited uploads plus MCP, burn-after-read, and custom expiration.

## Where are my screenshots stored?

On Cloudflare R2, with uploads served from a short domain (`snp.ink`). See the
[Privacy Policy](/legal/privacy) for retention and sub-processors.

## Can I delete a screenshot?

Yes — from the desktop app's History tab, or by hitting the API directly.
Burn-after-read uploads delete themselves after the first view. A nightly
cleanup job removes anything past its expiration.

## Will Claude/ChatGPT leak my screenshots?

LLM providers fetch the image when you paste the URL. SnapLink doesn't know
which LLM is fetching — the URL is just a public link. If the content is
sensitive, use burn-after-read or a 1-hour expiration.

## Does SnapLink work offline?

The watcher keeps running. Uploads queue until the network returns, then
flush in order. The URL arrives in your clipboard as soon as the upload
completes.

## Can I use my own short domain?

Team plan includes custom domains. Point a CNAME at `edge.snaplink.io` and
we'll issue a cert. Pro and Free use `snp.ink`.

## How do hotkeys work on Wayland?

Global hotkeys on Wayland are compositor-specific. SnapLink supports GNOME
and KDE via their respective shortcut APIs. On other compositors, fall back
to your compositor's keybinding config to invoke `snaplink --upload-clipboard`.

## Do you support screen-recording?

Not yet. SnapLink is image-only. Video is on the roadmap for v2.

## How is SnapLink different from imgur / 0x0.st / filebin?

- **Built for AI**: OCR is first-class, with a `.txt` endpoint on every URL.
- **Native desktop app**: zero-click uploads on screenshot capture.
- **MCP server**: Claude can search your screenshot history directly.
- **Real privacy**: EXIF stripping, burn-after-read, configurable expiration.

## Can I self-host?

Yes. The backend is MIT-licensed. Point the desktop app at your server via
Settings → Advanced → Upload endpoint. Self-hosted installs don't get our
cleanup automation or content moderation — you run what you deploy.
