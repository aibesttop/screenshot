## Maker Q&A (prep sheet)

**Why now?**
> LLMs accept image URLs natively. Until that changed, there was no point
> in optimizing the "screenshot → URL" path — you had to attach a file
> anyway. Now the clipboard-to-paste workflow is finally worth it.

**Why not imgur?**
> Imgur doesn't ship OCR, doesn't expire links, compresses images, and
> shows ads. SnapLink is the opposite.

**Is it open source?**
> Yes — the desktop app and backend are MIT on GitHub. You can self-host.
> The short domain and hosted infra are the proprietary part.

**What about privacy?**
> EXIF is stripped on upload. Every URL can be set to expire or
> burn-after-read. We don't train on uploads, don't serve ads, and never
> will.

**Roadmap?**
> Next 90 days: custom domains for Team, screen recording (short GIFs),
> and a CLI (`snaplink upload foo.png`) for CI pipelines.
